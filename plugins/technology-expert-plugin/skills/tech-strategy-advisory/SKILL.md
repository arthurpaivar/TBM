# Skill — Tech Strategy Advisory

**Expert technology strategy advisor with deep knowledge of frameworks used by top-tier consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG Platinion, Accenture, Bain) applied to payments technology leadership.** This skill provides authoritative, strategic guidance that translates technology decisions into business value. All outputs are executive-ready and designed for CIO-level conversations. It serves as the modular strategy knowledge base that consulting plugin uses for technology-specific advisory, communication plugin for strategy narratives, and finance plugin for investment analysis.

**Core Principles**: SPAR Framework, Pyramid Principle, SCQA Pattern, MECE Decomposition, Confidence Calibration, Source Attribution, Practical & Adaptable — these govern all outputs from this skill.

## Section 1 — McKinsey Digital Frameworks

### 1.1 McKinsey Technology Trends Outlook
McKinsey tracks 13+ frontier technology trends annually: Generative AI, Applied AI, Industrializing ML, Cloud and Edge Computing, Next-Gen Software Development, Digital Trust and Cybersecurity, Web3 and Digital Identity, Advanced Connectivity, Immersive Reality, Quantum Technologies, Electrification and Renewables, Climate Tech, Future of Mobility.

### 1.2 The 3Ds Framework
McKinsey's digital transformation model:
1. **Data**: Foundation of digital transformation — data architecture, analytics, AI/ML
2. **Design**: Human-centered design, customer journey mapping, experience optimization
3. **Distribution**: Omnichannel delivery, ecosystem partnerships, platform business models

### 1.3 Six Building Blocks for Digital Enterprise
1. **Strategy & Innovation**: Digital vision, innovation portfolio, ecosystem strategy
2. **Customer Design Journey**: End-to-end customer experience, design thinking
3. **Process Automation**: Intelligent automation, RPA, workflow optimization
4. **Technology Modernization**: Cloud migration, API-first architecture, data platforms
5. **Data & Analytics**: Data-driven decision making, advanced analytics, AI/ML
6. **Organization & Talent**: Digital skills, agile ways of working, culture change

### 1.4 Three Horizons Model
- **Horizon 1** (0–12 months): Protect and extend core business. Focus: operational efficiency, cost optimization, incremental improvements
- **Horizon 2** (12–36 months): Build emerging opportunities. Focus: new products, new markets, scaling pilots
- **Horizon 3** (36+ months): Explore transformative options. Focus: disruptive innovation, experimentation, strategic bets
- **Allocation Benchmark**: H1/H2/H3 split: 70/20/10 (mature) to 50/30/20 (innovation-led)

## Section 4 — Cross-Framework Strategic Tools

### 4.1 Target Operating Model (TOM) Design
Six components (MECE):
1. **Organization Structure**: Product vs. project orientation, CoE design
2. **Governance**: Decision rights, funding models, portfolio management
3. **Processes**: SDLC, change management, vendor management, demand management
4. **Technology**: Architecture principles, platform strategy, standards
5. **People & Culture**: Skills matrix, career paths, culture attributes, ways of working
6. **Sourcing**: Insource vs. outsource, managed services, partnerships

### 4.2 Product Operating Model
Shift from project-based to product-based IT:
- **From**: Temporary teams, project funding, deliverable-focused, handoffs between build and run
- **To**: Persistent product teams, value stream funding, outcome-focused, "you build it, you run it"
- **Key Enablers**: Platform engineering, team topologies, OKRs, lean portfolio management
- **Transition Path**: Start with 1-2 value streams → prove the model → scale gradually

### 4.3 Capability Mapping
Strategic tool for linking technology to business value:
- **Level 0**: Enterprise capabilities (e.g., "Payment Processing")
- **Level 1**: Business capabilities (e.g., "Transaction Authorization", "Settlement")
- **Level 2**: Component capabilities (e.g., "Fraud Screening", "Currency Conversion")
- Heat map by: maturity, strategic importance, investment level, risk

### 4.4 Value Stream Mapping
Optimize flow from idea to customer value:
- Map every step from demand to delivery
- Identify value-add vs. non-value-add activities
- Measure lead time, process time, % complete & accurate per step
- Target: Reduce total lead time by eliminating waste, handoffs, wait states

### 4.5 Technology Radar
Inspired by ThoughtWorks: Adopt/Trial/Assess/Hold quadrants across languages, platforms, tools, techniques. Update semi-annually.

### 4.6 Build vs. Buy vs. Partner

| Factor | Build | Buy | Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Best When** | Core differentiator, unique requirements | Commodity capability, mature market | Ecosystem play, speed to market |
| **Cost Profile** | High upfront, low ongoing | Low upfront, ongoing license/SaaS | Revenue share, integration costs |
| **Time to Value** | 6–18 months | 3–6 months | 1–3 months |
| **Control** | Full | Vendor dependent | Shared |
| **Risk** | Execution, talent dependency | Vendor lock-in, feature gaps | Partnership dependency |

### 4.7 Business Case Structure (Consulting Standard)
1. **Executive Summary**: The ask, the impact, the recommendation
2. **Strategic Context**: Market trends, competitive landscape, strategic imperatives
3. **Current State Assessment**: Pain points, capability gaps, opportunity cost
4. **Options Analysis**: 3–4 options with pros/cons/costs/risks
5. **Recommended Approach**: Detailed roadmap, phased delivery, milestones
6. **Financial Model**: NPV, IRR, payback period, TCO over 3–5 years
7. **Risk & Mitigation**: Key risks with likelihood, impact, mitigation
8. **Implementation Plan**: Timeline, dependencies, resources, governance
