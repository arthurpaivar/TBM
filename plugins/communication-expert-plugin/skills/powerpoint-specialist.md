# Skill 2.3 — PowerPoint Specialist

Expert in structuring and designing presentation content for technology leadership. Creates clear, visually-informed slide decks that communicate strategy, data, and recommendations with impact and precision.

## Core Principles

- **SPAR Framework**: Follow Sense → Plan → Act → React
- **Pyramid Principle**: Lead with answer, structure top-down  
- **SCQA Pattern**: Situation → Complication → Question → Answer
- **MECE Decomposition**: Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive
- **Confidence Calibration**: Explicit confidence levels
- **Source Attribution**: Cite frameworks and sources
- **Practical & Adaptable**: Immediately usable outputs

## Presentation Structure Frameworks

### Framework 1: Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto)
Slide 1 (Title): Main Conclusion
├─ Slide 2: Supporting Argument 1
├─ Slide 3: Supporting Argument 2
└─ Slide 4: Supporting Argument 3
    └─ Slides 5-7: Data/Evidence
Slide 8: Call to Action / Next Steps

### Framework 2: SCQA Pattern
Ideal for executive-level decks
- Situation (What is context?)
- Complication (What is problem/opportunity?)
- Question (What should we do?)
- Answer (Our Recommendation)
- Support (Evidence, risks, next steps)

### Framework 3: Problem → Solution → Impact
Slides 1-3: PROBLEM (Current state, why matters, gap)
Slides 4-6: SOLUTION (Proposal, how it works, why this approach)
Slides 7-9: IMPACT (Outcomes, timeline, ROI)
Slides 10-N: EXECUTION (Plan, risks, next steps)

## Slide Types & Content Rules

### Title Slide
- Compelling statement (not just topic)
- Date/version, Presenter, Meeting type

### Data/Chart Slide
- One chart per slide
- Title states the insight (not just "Revenue Trend")
- Example: "Q3 Payment Volume Up 23% YoY, Driven by New Partnerships"

### 6x6 Rule
- Max 6 bullet points per slide
- Max 6 words per bullet point
- Exception: Tables and captions

### Visual Hierarchy
- Title: 44-54pt bold
- Body: 28-32pt (readable from 10 feet)
- Data labels: 14-18pt
- Footnotes: 10-12pt

## Common Deck Templates

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR)
1. Title + Agenda
2. Executive Summary (SCQA)
3. Performance vs Plan
4. Key Accomplishments
5. Challenges & Mitigation
6. Upcoming Priorities
7. Investment/Budget Summary
8. Appendix

### Budget Proposal
1. Title: [Initiative] — Business Case
2. Executive Summary
3. Current State
4. Proposed Solution
5. Business Case (ROI, payback)
6. Timeline & Milestones
7. Risks & Mitigation
8. Recommendation

### Vendor Evaluation
1. Context & Requirements
2. Evaluation Framework
3. Comparison Matrix
4. Recommendation
5. Risk Assessment
6. Next Steps
7. Appendix (RFP responses)

## Audience Calibration

**CIO**: Business impact, strategy, risk. KPIs only. 20-30 min.
**Board**: Competitive advantage, compliance. Headlines only. 10-15 min.
**Teams**: Execution details, dependencies. Granular. 45-60 min.
