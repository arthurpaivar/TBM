# Skill — Tech BM Specialist — Technology Business Management

**Expert Technology Business Management practitioner with deep knowledge of TBM Framework 2.0 and TBM Taxonomy v5.0.1 (July 2025), the global standards maintained by the TBM Council.** This skill provides authoritative guidance on IT cost transparency, investment governance, FinOps, AI cost management, and value realization for a Payments CIO. It serves as the modular TBM knowledge base that other plugins reference — consulting for financial benchmarks, communication for cost narratives, technology for investment categories.

**Core Principles**: SPAR Framework, Pyramid Principle, SCQA Pattern, MECE Decomposition, Confidence Calibration, Source Attribution, Practical & Adaptable — these govern all outputs from this skill.

## Section 1 — TBM Framework 2.0

### 1.1 Framework Architecture
The modernized TBM Framework 2.0 (launched at TBM Council EMEA Summit, Amsterdam 2025) is organized as a vertically integrated structure connecting four layers:

1. **Foundations**: The base — what every organization needs to establish and sustain a TBM practice
2. **TBM Model**: The engine — the Taxonomy and allocation logic that maps costs to value
3. **TBM Outcomes**: The capabilities — transparency, alignment, optimization, innovation
4. **Organization Value Drivers**: The top — strategic outcomes that link IT to business value

### 1.2 TBM Foundations (5 Elements)
Before cost data can be modeled, organizations must establish:

1. **Roles**: Executive Sponsors, Practice Leads, Analysts, Administrators, Change Managers — the human capital to operate and evolve the model
2. **Data**: Financial data (GL), operational data (CMDB, monitoring), cloud data (billing APIs), HR data (headcount, rates), procurement data (contracts, vendors)
3. **Tools**: TBM platforms (Apptio/IBM, ServiceNow ITFM, Flexera, Cloudability) or spreadsheet-based models for early maturity
4. **Methods**: Standardized approaches for cost allocation, service modeling, showback/chargeback, benchmarking, scenario planning
5. **Change**: Organizational Change Management (OCM) — TBM must be actively used, not just technically sound

### 1.3 TBM Model
The TBM Model is the engine that enables transparency:
- Incorporates the **TBM Taxonomy v5.0.1** to structure cost flow
- Supports **Taxonomy Extensions** for industries (Banking, Manufacturing, Public Sector)
- Integrates **Connected Standards**: FinOps, NIST, ITFM, Agile, FAIR (cybersecurity), Sustainability/GreenOps

### 1.4 TBM Outcomes
Four organizational capabilities that emerge from effective TBM:
1. **Transparency**: Stakeholders see and understand technology costs and consumption
2. **Alignment**: Technology investments align with business strategy and priorities
3. **Optimization**: Continuous improvement of cost efficiency and resource utilization
4. **Innovation**: Freed capacity redirected to growth and transformation initiatives

### 1.5 Organization Value Drivers
The top of the framework — strategic outcomes:
- Business growth enablement
- Operational efficiency
- Risk reduction
- Customer experience improvement
- Competitive differentiation

### 1.6 Three Stakeholder Views
TBM data serves three audiences differently:
- **Finance View**: Cost pools and resource towers — budgeting, planning, chargeback
- **IT View**: Resource towers and solutions — capacity, utilization, performance
- **Business View**: Solutions and consumers — value delivery, consumption, ROI

## Section 2 — TBM Taxonomy v5.0.1 (July 2025)

### 2.1 Layer 1: Technology Cost Pools (Finance Layer)
Raw cost data from the general ledger, categorized by nature:

| Cost Pool | What It Captures | Examples |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Internal Labor** | FTE and contractor costs for IT staff | Salaries, benefits, bonuses |
| **External Labor** | Third-party staffing and professional services | Consultants, outsourced teams, SI partners |
| **Hardware** | Physical IT assets | Servers, storage arrays, network equipment |
| **Software** | Licensed and subscription software | Enterprise licenses, SaaS subscriptions |
| **Cloud Services** | Cloud provider spend (NEW emphasis in v5.0) | AWS, Azure, GCP — compute, storage, PaaS |
| **Facilities & Power** | Data center and office IT infrastructure | Colocation, power, cooling, floor space |
| **Telecom & Network** | Connectivity and communications | WAN, internet, VPN, voice |
| **Staffing** | Unified labor tracking (NEW in v5.0.1) | Headcount-based + spend-based views |
| **Other** | Miscellaneous IT expenses | Travel, training, supplies |

**v5.0.1 change**: Labor featured as both spend-based AND headcount-based cost pool. Cloud Services elevated to first-class cost pool for FinOps alignment.

### 2.2 Layer 2: Technology Resource Towers
Logical groupings of cost by technical function. **Resource Domains** introduced in v5.0.1 to group towers into strategic categories:

**Infrastructure Domain:**
| Tower | What It Models | Key Metrics |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Compute** | Servers, VMs, containers, serverless, AI compute (GPUs/TPUs) | Cost per vCPU-hour, utilization % |
| **Storage** | SAN, NAS, object, backup, AI training storage | Cost per TB, growth rate |
| **Network** | LAN, WAN, load balancers, CDN, AI-optimized networking | Cost per Gbps, latency |
| **End User** | Desktops, laptops, mobile, printing | Cost per device, refresh cycle |

**Application & Platform Domain:**
| Tower | What It Models | Key Metrics |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Application** | App hosting, maintenance, support (MOVED to Towers in v5.0.1) | Cost per application, health score |
| **Platform** | Middleware, integration, API gateways, AI/ML platforms | Cost per API call, platform adoption |
| **Smart Devices** (NEW v5.0) | IoT, edge computing, connected devices | Cost per device, data volume |

**Data Domain (NEW v5.0):**
| Tower | What It Models | Key Metrics |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Data Management** | Data governance, quality, master data | Cost per data domain |
| **Data Operations** | Data engineering, analytics platforms, pipelines | Cost per pipeline, data freshness |

**Security & Risk Domain (SPLIT in v5.0):**
| Tower | What It Models | Key Metrics |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Security** | Cybersecurity tools, SOC, vulnerability management | Cost per protected asset |
| **Risk & Compliance** | GRC, audit, regulatory compliance | Cost per compliance control |

**Management Domain:**
| Tower | What It Models | Key Metrics |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Delivery** | Project management, BA, testing, agile teams | Cost per story point, per sprint |
| **IT Management** | IT governance, ITSM, service desk | Cost per ticket, per incident |
| **IT Human Capital** (NEW v5.0) | Training, talent development, workforce planning | Cost per FTE, training hours |

### 2.3 Layer 3: Technology Solutions (Services Layer)
Business-facing products and services consumed by stakeholders:
- IT services, business applications, digital products, platforms
- **NEW in v5.0.1**: AI Solutions and Sustainability Solutions as new solution types
- Note: Applications moved from Solutions to Towers in v5.0.1 (significant modeling change)

### 2.4 Layer 4: Technology Consumers (Business Unit / Capability Layer)
Cost by business outcome:
- Business units, cost centers, product lines, customer segments
- **NEW in v5.0.1**: Expanded to include **value streams, products, and portfolios** — supporting outcomes-based funding models

## Section 3 — Cost Allocation Models

### 3.1 Allocation Methods

| Method | Accuracy | Complexity | Best For |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Direct** | High | Low | Costs clearly attributable to one consumer |
| **Usage-Based** | High | High | Consumption-measurable services (cloud, API calls, transactions) |
| **Weighted** | Medium | Medium | Shared costs allocated by proxy (headcount, revenue, txn volume) |
| **Even-Split** | Low | Low | Truly shared costs (use sparingly) |

For payments technology, **transaction volume** is typically the strongest allocation driver.

### 3.2 Showback vs. Chargeback

| Aspect | Showback | Chargeback |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **P&L Impact** | None — reporting only | Formal cost transfer to BU P&L |
| **Behavior Change** | Moderate — awareness drives some optimization | Strong — financial accountability drives discipline |
| **Complexity** | Lower — approximate allocations acceptable | Higher — requires defensible, auditable allocations |
| **Maturity Required** | Walk level | Run level |

Maturity path: Basic Reporting → Showback → Activity-Based Chargeback → Consumption-Based Chargeback

### 3.3 Payments-Specific Allocation
Allocate by:
- Payment rail (ACH, wire, card, RTP/FedNow)
- Product line (merchant acquiring, issuing, treasury, correspondent banking)
- Channel (branch, digital, API, partner)
- Geography (domestic, cross-border, by region)

## Section 4 — Investment Management

### 4.1 Run / Grow / Transform Model
Classify technology spend:

| Category | Definition | Target % | Typical % |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Run** | Maintaining current services, BAU, compliance | <55% (best-in-class) | 60–70% |
| **Grow** | Enhancing existing capabilities, scaling products | 20–30% | 15–25% |
| **Transform** | New capabilities, innovation, digital transformation | 15–25% | 10–15% |

**Benchmark** (Gartner): Best-in-class 55/25/20. Industry average 65/20/15.

### 4.2 Investment Typification
Beyond Run/Grow/Transform, classify investments by:
- **Strategic alignment**: Which business OKR does it serve?
- **Value type**: Cost reduction, revenue generation, risk mitigation, experience improvement
- **Horizon**: H1 (maintain), H2 (grow), H3 (transform) — aligned with McKinsey Three Horizons
- **Mandatory vs. discretionary**: Regulatory/compliance (non-negotiable) vs. business choice

### 4.3 Business Case Framework
Standard structure for technology investment cases:
1. **Strategic alignment**: Link to business strategy and OKRs
2. **Cost-benefit analysis**: NPV, IRR, payback period
3. **Total Cost of Ownership**: 3–5 year view including run costs
4. **Risk assessment**: Technical, delivery, adoption, regulatory
5. **Alternatives analysis**: Build vs. buy vs. partner
6. **Value realization plan**: Defined metrics, milestone gates, post-implementation reviews

### 4.4 Value Realization
Track that investments deliver promised benefits:
- Define value metrics at approval (cost savings, revenue uplift, risk reduction)
- Milestone-based funding with go/no-go gates
- Post-implementation reviews at 6 and 12 months
- Value leakage analysis: planned vs. actual benefits
- Continuous value tracking dashboard

## Section 5 — Connected Standards & Modern Extensions

### 5.1 FinOps Integration
TBM Framework 2.0 formally connects with FinOps:
- Cloud unit economics (cost per workload, per environment, per team)
- Tagging and labeling standards aligned to TBM taxonomy layers
- Three FinOps phases: Inform → Optimize → Operate
- Shared responsibility model: Finance + Engineering + Cloud Platform teams
- Key FinOps KPIs: commitment coverage rate, waste score, cost per deployment, cloud cost growth vs. business growth

### 5.2 AI Cost Management
As AI adoption grows, TBM must capture:
- **GPU/TPU compute**: Training vs. inference cost separation
- **Model licensing**: API consumption costs (per token, per call)
- **Data preparation**: Labeling, cleaning, augmentation costs
- **AI platform**: MLOps infrastructure, experiment tracking, model registry
- **Governance**: Model risk management, validation, monitoring costs
- Map to Taxonomy: AI-specific sub-towers in Compute, Storage, Network, Platform (v5.0.1)

### 5.3 GreenOps / Sustainability
TBM extension for ESG reporting:
- Carbon footprint per IT tower and service
- Green cloud metrics: PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness), renewable energy %
- Sustainability scoring in investment decisions
- Scope 1/2/3 emissions tracking for technology operations

### 5.4 FAIR Framework (Cybersecurity Risk)
TBM + FAIR alignment for communicating cyber risk in financial terms:
- Risk quantification in dollar terms (annualized loss expectancy)
- Security investment ROI connected to risk reduction
- Cyber risk as an IT cost category within the TBM model

## Section 6 — TBM Maturity Model

### 6.1 Classic Maturity Levels (TBM Council)

| Level | Characteristics | Key Indicators |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Crawl** | Basic cost reporting, spreadsheet-based, limited allocation | GL data only, no tower mapping, manual processes |
| **Walk** | Automated data collection, tower-level allocation, showback | TBM tool in place, basic showback, monthly reporting |
| **Run** | Full taxonomy, chargeback, real-time dashboards, decision-grade | Consumption-based chargeback, unit economics, scenario planning |

### 6.2 Maturity Assessment 2.0 (2025)
The new TBM Maturity Assessment 2.0 shifts from static levels to a **4×4 quadrant model**:
- Connects maturity directly to TBM Outcomes (Transparency, Alignment, Optimization, Innovation)
- Continuous improvement tool, not a compliance checklist
- Led by TBM Council and Rego Consulting

### 6.3 IT Spend Benchmarks

| Metric | Financial Services | Cross-Industry |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| IT spend as % of revenue | 7–10% | 3–5% |
| Cloud spend as % of IT | 25–35% (growing) | 20–30% |
| Run/Grow/Transform | 65/20/15 (avg) | 70/20/10 (avg) |
| IT staff as % of total | 8–12% | 5–8% |
| Cost per payment txn | Varies by rail | N/A |
| External labor % | 35–45% | 25–35% |

Sources: Gartner IT Spending Forecasts, TBM Council Benchmarks, FinOps Foundation.
